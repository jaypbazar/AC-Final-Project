Symmertric Encryption Algorithms

Caesar Cipher

An encryption algorithm that divides plaintext into fixed-size blocks and encrypts each block using a symmetric key.

Vernam Cipher

The Vernam cipher, also known as a one-time pad, is a method of encrypting alphabetic text using a substitution technique.

Block Cipher

A substitution cipher that shifts each letter in the plaintext by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet.


Asymmertric Encryption Algorithms

RSA Encryption

RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) is a widely used asymmetric encryption algorithm that relies on the computational difficulty of factoring the product of two large prime numbers.

ECC Encryption

ECC (Elliptic Curve Cryptography) is a more modern approach to asymmetric cryptography that relies on the algebraic structure of elliptic curves over finite fields.


Hashing Algorithms

MD5

A widely-used cryptographic hash function that produces a 128-bit hash value, but is now considered insecure due to vulnerability to collision attacks.

SHA-1

Generates a 160-bit hash value and was commonly used in security protocols, but is deprecated because of weaknesses allowing for collision attacks.

SHA-256

A part of the SHA-2 family, producing a 256-bit hash, and is widely used for cryptographic security, offering strong resistance to collisions.

SHA-512

Another member of the SHA-2 family, producing a 512-bit hash value, and provides stronger security and greater resistance to attacks compared to SHA-256.
